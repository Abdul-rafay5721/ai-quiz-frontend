# Studies in the Gospels

The Gospels—Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John—narrate the life, ministry, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ. Each Gospel presents a unique perspective and emphasizes different aspects of Jesus' teachings and miracles.

Each Gospel writer presents a unique perspective and emphasis, reflecting different audiences and theological themes.

Studying the Gospels helps us understand the teachings of Jesus, His miracles, His compassion, and His fulfillment of Old Testament prophecies.

These accounts serve as the foundation of Christian faith and practice, revealing Jesus as the Son of God and the Savior of the world.

## Gospel of Matthew

Matthew presents Jesus as the promised Messiah, the King of the Jews. He emphasizes Jesus' fulfillment of Old Testament prophecies and the establishment of the Kingdom of God.

**Scriptural Reference:**  
- Matthew 28:18-20  
- Matthew 5-7 (Sermon on the Mount)

## Gospel of Mark

Mark portrays Jesus as the suffering Servant who came to give His life as a ransom for many. His Gospel emphasizes Jesus' deeds and the need for discipleship and faith.

**Scriptural Reference:**  
- Mark 10:45  
- Mark 1:16-20 (Calling of the Disciples)

## Gospel of Luke

Luke presents Jesus as the compassionate Savior who came to seek and save the lost. He highlights Jesus' ministry to the marginalized and the universal scope of salvation.

**Scriptural Reference:**  
- Luke 19:10  
- Luke 15 (Parables of the Lost Sheep, Coin, and Son)

## Gospel of John

John emphasizes Jesus' divine nature as the Son of God. His Gospel contains profound theological insights and highlights Jesus' miracles and "I am" statements.

**Scriptural Reference:**  
- John 1:1-18  
- John 14:6 ("I am the Way, the Truth, and the Life")
