# Studies in General Epistles

The General Epistles consist of seven letters: **James, 1 Peter, 2 Peter, 1 John, 2 John, 3 John, and Jude**. These letters address various aspects of Christian faith and practice, offering valuable teachings on Christian living, ethics, and theology.

The epistles reflect the diversity of early Christian thought and provide guidance on faith, community life, and navigating the relationship between Christianity and broader society.

Studying the General Epistles helps believers understand how different authors approached the challenges of faith and practice, offering timeless wisdom for living out the Christian life.

---

## Key Themes and Scriptures

### Faith and Works (James)
- **James 2:14, 17**:  
  *"What good is it, my brothers and sisters, if someone claims to have faith but has no deeds? Can such faith save them? ... In the same way, faith by itself, if it is not accompanied by action, is dead."*

---

### Hope and Endurance (1 Peter)
- **1 Peter 1:3-4**:  
  *"Praise be to the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ! In his great mercy he has given us new birth into a living hope through the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead, and into an inheritance that can never perish, spoil or fade."*

---

### False Teachers and God’s Judgment (2 Peter)
- **2 Peter 2:1**:  
  *"But there were also false prophets among the people, just as there will be false teachers among you."*

---

### Love and Obedience (1 John)
- **1 John 4:7-8**:  
  *"Dear friends, let us love one another, for love comes from God. Everyone who loves has been born of God and knows God. Whoever does not love does not know God, because God is love."*

---

### Walking in Truth (2 John)
- **2 John 1:4**:  
  *"It has given me great joy to find some of your children walking in the truth, just as the Father commanded us."*

---

### Hospitality and Contending for the Faith (3 John)
- **3 John 1:5-6**:  
  *"Dear friend, you are faithful in what you are doing for the brothers and sisters, even though they are strangers to you. They have told the church about your love."*

---

### Contending for the Faith (Jude)
- **Jude 1:3**:  
  *"Dear friends, although I was very eager to write to you about the salvation we share, I felt compelled to write and urge you to contend for the faith that was once for all entrusted to God’s holy people."*
